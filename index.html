Benjamin Ballyk

I'm Ben, a DPhil student in Engineering Science at the University of Oxford, based at the Oxford Robotics Institute. I'm supervised by Oiwi Parker Jones and Ingmar Posner, as part of the Parker Jones Neural Processing Lab (PNPL) and the Applied AI Lab (A2I).

Previously, I completed an MSc in Mathematical Modelling & Scientific Computing at Oxford, and a dual undergraduate degree in Mechanical Engineering (Western University) and Business Administration (Ivey Business School) in Canada.

Research

My work centers on decoding speech directly from noninvasive recordings of brain activity. In particular, I develop biologically-grounded, physics-informed generative models — using flow-matching to synthesize realistic, cross-patient neural data — that help speech-decoding brain-computer interfaces generalize across people, even when real neural data is scarce. In the past, I've also dabbled in the world of differentially private data generation, and efficient vision-language models (VLMs) for self-driving cars.
